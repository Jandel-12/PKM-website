BRIEF HISTORY OF PAMBAYANG KOLEHIYO NG MAUBAN

College education has always been elusive for the Filipinos at large. Thus, it is an accepted maxim that education at least in the tertiary level, is a privilege and not a right. But with the establishment of the Pambayang Kolehiyo ng Mauban (PKM), college education is not an impossible dream anymore among the young people of Mauban. With its creation by the virtue of Sangguniang Bayan Ordinance No. 05, S-2005 all Maubanins state their rightful claim to quality education. It all started with the observation of Mayor Fernando Q. Llamas that more and more high school graduates from Mauban were asking scholarship grants from him, One day, he asked his finance advisers if the fiscal condition of the town could sustain the operation of a municipal college. The resounding answer was yes! With Mauban's Special Education Fund (SEF) experiencing robust growth, there will be no problem establishing a free tertiary education in Mauban. Preliminary works started in 2003. Professors from the University of the Philippines-Diliman were consulted with regard to the organizational structure, curricular offerings and human resource recruitment. After a series of seminars, meetings and interviews, faculty members and staff were hired to constitute the pioneering workforce of the college. And in June 2005, the Pambayang Kolehiyo ng Mauban started its mandate of providing low-cost quality education to the young people of Mauban. It initially offers Bachelor of Elementary Education and Bachelor of Secondary Education Major in English, Filipino, Mathematics. PKM also offered in June 2006 Programming NC Level IV and in June 2008 Computer Hardware Servicing under TESDA Programs. Government Recognition No. 02, Series of 2010 was granted in accordance with the pertinent provisions of Republic Act No. 7722, otherwise known as "Higher Education Act of 1994," and by virtue of resolution No. 153-2000, Series of 2000, of the Commission en banc to offer Bachelor of Elementary Education effective Academic Year 2009-2010. Likewise, Government Recognition No. 03, Series of 2010 was granted in accordance with the pertinent provisions of Republic Act No. 7722, otherwise known as "Higher Education Act of 1994," and by virtue of resolution No. 153-2000, Series of 2000, of the Commission en banc to offer Bachelor of Secondary Education Majors in English, Filipino, Mathematics effective Academic Year 2009-2010. For Academic Year 2011-2012, PKM offers Bachelor of Elementary Education, Bachelor of Secondary Education Major in English, Filipino, Mathematics, Programming NC Level IV, Computer Hardware Servicing NC Level II, and Agricultural Crops Production NC Level II courses. At present, PKM is occupying the two sites in conduct of its daily operation; one in Sitio Pilaway, Brgy. Polo for Education Programs and one in Brgy. Soledad for TESDA Programs.

Academic Policies

Section I. Admission Requirements

For Freshmen

  • Original Form 138
  • Good moral certificate
  • 4 copies of latest picture (2 x 2)
  • Original (NSO) Birth Certificate
  • Barangay Clearance
  • Medical Certificate with Chest X-ray result/Sputum Test

For Transferees

  • Transfer Credentials
  • Original Scholastic record bearing school seal and signature of registrar
  • Good character testimony from the authorized official of former school
  • 4 copies of latest picture (2 x 2)
  • Original (NSO) Birth Certificate
  • Barangay Clearance
  • Medical Certificate with Chest X-ray result/Sputum Test

Enrollment of Married Female Students

  • Must present the original copy of her marriage contract
  • Admission will be denied without the marriage certificate
  • Pregnant students expected to give birth within the semester shall not be allowed to enroll
  • Students who have given birth may return upon submission of appropriate medical clearance

Admission and Enrollment Procedures

  1. Proceed to the registrar section for application and placement for the entrance examination
  2. Submit duly accomplished entrance examination form and 2 (2 x 2) I.D. pictures
  3. Get the schedule of examination and room assignment

Section II. Retention Policies, Scholastic Delinquency, and Disciplinary Policies

A. Retention Policies

  • A student who incurs failing grades in three subjects (including NSTP) within a semester will be placed on academic probation.
  • A student who takes an absence without an official leave will not be allowed to re-enroll.

B. Leave of Absence (LOA) & Residency

  • Students must file a Leave of Absence (LOA) or enroll for residency.
  • A student is entitled to LOA for a period not exceeding one year.
  • LOA must be approved by the Area Chair and the College Dean.
  • Students who leave the college without an approved LOA will be denied re-admission unless granted an exception by the Dean for valid reasons.

C. Scholastic Delinquency

  1. Warning: Any student who obtains final grades below "3" in 25% to 49% of the total number of academic units registered for the semester shall be warned by the Dean to improve his/her work based on the recommendation of the Area Chair.
  2. Probation: Any student who obtains final grades below "3" in 50% to 75% of the total academic units shall be placed on probation the succeeding semester. The student's load shall be reduced as determined by the Dean based on the Area Chair's recommendation.
    Probation may be removed by passing with grades of "3" or better in more than 50% of the units during the succeeding semester.
  3. Dismissal: Any student who obtains final grades below "3" in more than 75% but less than 100% of the total academic units shall be dropped from the rolls of the college.
    A student dropped from one college shall NOT be admitted to another unit unless the Dean of Students finds the student's aptitude suitable for a different field of study.
  4. Permanent Disqualification: Any student who obtains final grades below "3" in 100% of the academic units shall be permanently barred from readmission to the college.

D. Honorable Dismissal

A student in good standing who wishes to sever connection with the College shall present a written petition to the College Registrar signed by a parent or guardian.
If granted, the student will receive an honorable dismissal, which indicates voluntary withdrawal in good standing regarding character and conduct.
All college indebtedness must be settled before issuance.
If the student was dropped due to poor scholarship, this may be noted on the honorable dismissal.

E. Disciplinary Policies

A PKM student found guilty of a major offense three (3) times in a semester shall NOT be eligible for re-enrollment (Article VIII E).

A transferee is automatically placed on disciplinary probation. If he/she commits a major offense, the student shall NOT be allowed to re-enroll.
